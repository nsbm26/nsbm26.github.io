Call for Papers

Call for Papers

The Concept and Application of Bioactive Molecules (CAMBio) Laboratory, in partnership with the National Higher School of Biotechnology (ENSB) – Taoufik Khaznadar, is pleased to invite researchers, academics, PhD students, and professionals to submit their original contributions to the:

1st Hybrid National Seminar on Bioactive Molecules

From Characterization to Innovative Biotechnological Applications

📅 Seminar Dates: November 29–30, 2026

📍 Venue: National Higher School of Biotechnology (ENSB), Constantine, Algeria

🌐 Format: Hybrid (On-site & Online)

Scope

The seminar aims to promote scientific exchange and interdisciplinary collaboration in the field of bioactive molecules and their innovative biotechnological applications.

We welcome original research contributions related, but not limited, to the following themes.

Scientific Topics

🩺 Topic 1

Pharmacology of Bioactive Molecules and Citizen Health

  • Drug discovery
  • Pharmacology
  • Toxicology
  • Therapeutic applications
  • Medicinal chemistry

🌱 Topic 2

Bioactive Molecules for Food Security and Agro-industry

  • Functional foods
  • Food biotechnology
  • Natural preservatives
  • Agricultural biotechnology
  • Sustainable food production

🔬 Topic 3

Microbial Biotechnologies and Energy Security

  • Industrial microbiology
  • Fermentation technologies
  • Biofuels
  • Renewable energy
  • Environmental microbiology

🌍 Topic 4

Environmental Valorization and Preservation

  • Bioremediation
  • Circular bioeconomy
  • Waste valorization
  • Green technologies
  • Environmental biotechnology

🤖 Topic 5

Digital Innovation and Artificial Intelligence in Biotechnology

  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Machine Learning
  • Bioinformatics
  • Computational biology
  • Digital biotechnology

Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit an original abstract presenting unpublished research.

Submitted abstracts should

  • be written in English or French;
  • clearly state the objectives, methodology, results, and conclusions;
  • correspond to one of the seminar themes;
  • represent original work that has not been previously published.

Detailed formatting instructions and the abstract template will be available soon.
